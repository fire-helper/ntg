Social comparison theory

History

Leon Festinger (1919 - 1989)

  • Has roots in The psychology of status (Herbert Hyman, 1942)
  • Proposed in 1954
  • Developed by many people later on

The theory

as theorized by Leon Festinger
  • Humans evaluate themselves through objective means
  • If not possible, humans evaluate themselves through comparison
  • Comparisons happen against the most relatable people

Later on, there are new motives for social comparison:

  • Self-enhancement
  • Self-improvement

Types of social comparison

  • Upward comparison
  • Downward comparison

Upward comparison

Compare against those who are better-off. Double-edged blade.

  • → Motivation, self-improvement
  • → Lower self-esteem because of impossible model

Downward comparison

Compare against those who are worse-off

→ Maintain self-esteem, self-enhancement

Effects on you

Social media allows constant exposure to upward social comparison.

→ Lower self-esteem

Vogel (2014). https://doi.org/10.1037/ppm0000047

Effects on you

Advertisements show people idealized, perfect imagery.

→ Distort consumers' standards

Richins (1991). https://doi.org/10.1086/209242

Effects on you

Marketing applies downward comparison on existing consumers, pushing them to chase trends and newer products.

"Do you have the latest iPhone model yet?"

Moral of the story: try to compare properly
